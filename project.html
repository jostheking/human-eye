HUMAN EYE

PART OF EYE :



SCLERA :

It is the outermost covering of the eye . It is made of white tough fibrous tissues . It protects the vital internal parts of eye from external injuries .



CHOROID :

The dark grey pigmented layer attached to the sclera on its inner side is called the choroid . It is richly supplied with blood vessels . It darkens the eye from inside and hence , prevents any internal reflection



cornea :

The sclera layer continues in the front of the eye as cornea . It is tye transparent white portion of eye that covers the transparent bulge on the surface of the eyeball . It allows the light to enter into the eyeball .



RETINA :

It is the innermost delicate membrane having a large number of cells called "rods" and "cones" . The cones sensitive to bright lights and can detect colour,whereas rods or senitive to dim lights .



IRIS AND PUPIL :

Behind the cornea is the iris . It lends colour to the eye . The amount of pigmentation in the iris gives colour to the eyes . When the pigmentation is the highest , the iris is black or brown in colour . When the pigmentation is least ,the iris is blue in colour . the iris has a central circular aperture called pupil . The iris regulates the amount of light entering the eye by adjusting the size of the pupil in the following ways :


(a) If the light is dim , the iris expands the pupil in order to allow more light to enter the eye .


(b) If the light is bright , the iris contracts in order to make the pupil decrease the amount of light entering the eye . The iris consists of muscles that expand and contract the pupil .



CRYSTALLINE LENS :

It is a transparent crystalline double convex lens situated just behind the iris . It is held in its position with the help of ciliary muscles .



SUSPENSORY LIGAMENTS :

Along with the ciliary muscles , suspensory ligaments are also attached to the lens .



CILIARY MUSCLES :

Thes are the muscles that hold the eye lens in the proper place . The focal length of the eye lens can be changed with the help of ciliary muscles according to the distance of the object in front of the eye . When the ciliary muscles contract , the eye lens become thick and its focal length decreases . When the ciliary muscles are relaxed , the eye lens become thin and its focal length increases .



OPTIC NERVE :

Optic nerve consist of about one million separate nerve fibers that connect the rods and cones of the retina to the brain . Its function is to carry optical messages in the form of electic signals to the brain .



AQUEOUS HUMOUR :

The space of the eye between cornea and the eye lens is filed with a clear watery fluid which is known as aqueous humour .



VITREOUS FLUID :

The space between retina and the eye lens is filled with a fluid called vitreous fluid . It keeps the eye wet . It is mainly composed of water . It occupies two-third of the volume of the eye .



BLIND SPOT :

At the junction of the optic nerves and the retina , there are no sensory nerves (rods or cones) . So , no vision is possible at that spot . This is called the blind spot of the eye .
